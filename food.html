Island Foods

Food is one of the easiest ways to feel at home on an island. Many dishes are simple but filling, using ingredients that come from the ocean, the land, or backyard gardens. The table below lists a few common foods you might see across different Pacific islands.

Sample Pacific Island Foods
Food Main Ingredient Island Region Notes
Lu Pulu Taro leaves and corned beef Tonga Baked in coconut cream, often served at big gatherings.
Kalua Pig Pork Hawai ªi Slow cooked in an underground oven called an imu.
Kokoda Fish and coconut cream Fiji Similar to ceviche, marinated with lime and fresh vegetables.
Panipopo Sweet bread and coconut sauce Samoa Soft rolls baked and covered in a sweet coconut mixture.

These dishes are just a small taste of what each island offers. Every family has its own favorite version of these foods, and recipes are often passed down by watching and helping in the kitchen instead of reading from a book.
